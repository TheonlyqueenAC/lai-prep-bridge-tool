🔬 Critical Insights: Where Barriers Occur

Oral PrEP: Post-Initiation Adherence Problem

The critical barrier occurs after prescription and initiation. Patients can start same-day, but 40-48% discontinue due to daily pill burden, side effects, and adherence challenges. Trans women face 70% discontinuation—the highest of any group.

LAI-PrEP: Pre-Initiation Access Problem

The critical barrier occurs before the first injection. Conservative initiation protocols create a mandatory 2-8 week bridge period where 47.1% are lost to HIV testing, insurance authorization, and appointment coordination. Those who successfully navigate show 81-83% persistence.

The Implementation Paradox

LAI-PrEP was designed to solve oral PrEP's adherence problem, and it succeeds: 81-83% retention vs. 52% for oral. However, it creates a new problem—nearly half of motivated users are eliminated by structural barriers before experiencing this advantage.

Population Disparities

Oral PrEP shows dramatic population differences: trans women (70% loss), young Black MSM (69% loss), and those without insurance face highest attrition. LAI-PrEP's bridge period affects all populations equally (47.1%)—it's a structural, not behavioral, barrier.
